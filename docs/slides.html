CartGuard

Stop risky cosmetics claims before they go live.

Developer-first claim checks in CI with source-linked evidence.

Problem

AI content is scaling faster than compliance review.

  • More AI-written product copy every week
  • Manual review is slow and inconsistent
  • Weak claims can reach production
  • Teams struggle to prove claim origin quickly

Real Company Case

L'Oréal: reported €189.5M French antitrust fine (appeal dismissed in 2023).

Signal for our market: compliance failures in consumer/beauty categories can create very large financial downside.

Real Company Case

L'Oréal + peers: reported ~€10M Turkish settlement (collective).

  • Theme: resale pricing and sales restrictions allegations
  • Also reported: €90K French penalty for payment delay compliance issue

Real Company Case

L'Oréal (US): FTC challenged anti-aging "clinically proven" claims.

Key lesson: unsupported marketing claims trigger regulatory action even without a giant fine. Trust and substantiation matter.

Why This Keeps Happening

  • Compliance sits too late in content workflow
  • Evidence quality is not machine-checked
  • Cross-border policy differences create rule drift
  • No deterministic pre-publish gate for claims

Solution

CartGuard = CI for claims.

  • Typed claim schema
  • Policy-as-code validation
  • Source-linked evidence output
  • Pass/fail gate before publish
