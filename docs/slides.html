Developer-first TypeScript SDK for APAC teams entering Europe: flag missing evidence, return recommendations, ship faster.

Why Now

4.6B imports. 91% from China. This is the EU entry bottleneck.

  • Cross-border volume is exploding faster than manual review teams can scale.
  • EU enforcement is getting stricter and more visible every quarter.
  • Every new country launch multiplies legal, language, and claim risk.
  • The winner will be the team that catches missing evidence before go-live.

Signal #1

EU low-value ecommerce imports exploded in 3 years.

  • 2022: 1.4B consignments
  • 2023: 2.3B consignments
  • 2024: 4.6 billion (12 million/day)
  • 2025 estimate: 5.8B (+26% vs 2024)
  • Share from China in 2024: 91%

Stage-safe source: European Commission + EU customs facts pages.

Signal #2

EU ecommerce adoption keeps rising.

  • Enterprises selling online: 17.2% (2013) -> 23.8% (2023)
  • Share of turnover from web sales in 2023: 7.32%
  • Own websites/apps: 6.25%
  • Marketplaces: 1.06%

Implication: more product pages, more claims, more compliance load.

Signal #3

EU regulatory pressure is real and active.

  • Digital Services Act: fines up to 6% of global annual turnover.
  • GDPR: up to EUR 20M or 4% of global turnover.
  • Competition penalties can reach 10% of annual worldwide turnover.
  • For cross-border entrants, this is a board-level risk.

These are legal caps, not theoretical blog numbers.

Evidence

Public enforcement already shows financial impact.

  • Italy AGCM (2025): EUR 1M fine tied to misleading/omissive claims (Shein case).
  • France DGCCRF (2025): reported EUR 40M enforcement action for deceptive practices.
  • EU CPC network: coordinated actions against major marketplaces.
  • Bottom line: evidence checks are no longer optional.

All numbers should be re-verified before legal/investor materials.

Definition

What is a claim?

  • A claim is any product statement that can affect trust and regulatory review.
  • Examples: "clinically proven", "eco-friendly", "non-toxic", "dermatologist tested".
  • If required support is missing, the claim should be reviewed before launch.
  • CartGuard treats each claim as a typed, checkable object.

Product Fit

CartGuard is CI for cross-border evidence completeness.

  • Step 1: Parse product content into claim objects.
  • Step 2: Validate against country policy pack (EU first).
  • Step 3: Flag missing required evidence/documents by rule.
  • Step 4: Return recommendation report for engineering and legal review.
