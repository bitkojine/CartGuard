Reality Check

No single public dataset shows exactly how many companies "migrate" to Europe.

  • There is no central EU registry for all APAC ecommerce entrants.
  • So we use hard proxies: parcel volume, trade flows, and FDI project counts.
  • These proxies show strong growth in cross-border market entry pressure.
  • This is enough signal to justify a focused compliance infrastructure wedge.

Market Signal #1

EU low-value ecommerce imports: 1.4B -> 2.3B -> 4.6B -> 5.8B items.

  • 2022: 1.4 billion
  • 2023: 2.3 billion
  • 2024: 4.6 billion (12 million/day)
  • 2025: 5.8 billion (official estimate)
  • 2024 share from China: 91%

Source: European Commission and EU customs facts pages (2025 updates).

Market Signal #2

10-year trade trend: EU imports from China doubled.

  • EU imports from China in 2024: EUR 517.8B
  • Change vs 2014: +101.9%
  • This is structural, long-cycle cross-border commercial volume.
  • Where volume grows this fast, compliance mistakes scale too.

Source: Eurostat (published March 4, 2025).

Market Signal #3

Company movement proxy: APAC investment projects into Europe are active.

  • In Germany alone (2023): 336 Asia-Pacific greenfield/expansion projects.
  • Chinese FDI into EU+UK in 2024: EUR 10B (+47% YoY).
  • First annual increase since 2016 in that China-to-Europe FDI series.
  • Signal: companies are still entering Europe, even in difficult macro cycles.

Source: Germany Trade & Invest (2024 release), MERICS + Rhodium (2025 update).

Regulatory Gap

Europe has hard penalty regimes that many entrants underestimate.

  • EU competition rules: fines can reach 10% of worldwide annual turnover.
  • GDPR: up to EUR 20M or 4% of global annual turnover (whichever higher).
  • Digital Services Act: up to 6% of global annual turnover for non-compliance.
  • Bottom line: EU entry risk is a board-level financial risk, not just legal admin.

Source: European Commission competition policy, GDPR Article 83, DSA framework.

Pain We Solve

Why mid-sized APAC teams fail EU launch checks.

  • They can write content fast, but cannot prove claim evidence on demand.
  • Legal review happens late, after translations and listing rollout.
  • Rules differ by market and drift across teams.
  • Manual checks break at scale when SKU and country count rises.

Product Fit

CartGuard is CI for market-entry claims.

  • Validate every claim object against explicit policy rules.
  • Require source links for high-risk categories before publish.
  • Output machine-readable pass/fail + audit trail artifacts.
  • Integrate in CI so risky product pages are blocked automatically.
