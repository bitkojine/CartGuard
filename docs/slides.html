CartGuard

Stop risky cosmetics claims before they go live.

Developer-first claim checks in CI with source-linked evidence.

Problem

Cosmetics marketing risk is expensive.

  • More AI-written product copy every week
  • Manual review is slow and inconsistent
  • Weak or unsupported claims can reach production
  • When issues hit regulators, the cost can be huge

Real Company Example

Reported L'Oréal figure: €189.5M (French antitrust case).

This is the scale of downside that exists in beauty and consumer markets when compliance issues become regulatory cases.

Real Company Example

  • Also reported: €90K French penalty for payment-delay compliance issue

Real Company Example

L'Oréal (US): FTC challenged anti-aging "clinically proven" claims.

Key lesson: even without a giant monetary penalty, unsupported claims create direct legal risk and forced marketing changes.

Marketing Compliance Numbers

Reported penalties are often in millions, not thousands.

  • US FTC deceptive marketing settlements cited in the $10M-$43M range
  • FTC fake review rule: up to $53,088 per violation (reported cap)
  • Large EU competition cases in consumer sectors can reach hundreds of millions

What Fails In Teams

Why these losses keep repeating

  • Legal review starts too late
  • Claim evidence quality is inconsistent
  • Cross-border rules are hard to track
  • No pre-publish gate in engineering workflow

Solution

CartGuard = CI for claims.

  • Typed claim schema
  • Policy-as-code validation
  • Source-linked evidence output
  • Pass/fail gate before publish
